Rules

Pitchstorm is a party game that puts you in the position of an unprepared writer pitching movies to the world's worst executives (AKA, your friends.)

Each round, one player acts as the executive, while everyone else plays as a writer trying to pitch them the next Hollywood Blockbuster.

Writer: Open the "Writer" tab and get new prompts (character, plot, and an award you're aiming for). You can click on any of the prompts once if you want to swap it for a different one. Read the prompts out loud, the executive starts a timer, and you have 40 seconds to pitch your next masterpiece. After that the executive will give you a "helpful" note, and you will have 20 more seconds to incorporate this terrible idea into the story.

Executive: Use the timer to time writer's pitch. Once the author is out of time, interrupt them and give them one of your notes, and set the timer for 20 more seconds.

After everyone has pitched a movie, the executive chooses which one they want to make, and that player gets a point. The next player becomes an executive, and you play the next round. Keep playing until everyone has pitched 3 moives. The person with the most points wins and becomes rich and famous!
